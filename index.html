About Me

Yoda said, "do or do not, there is no try"....that is about the most inspirational thing I've ever heard! granted it's from a movie but hey! It has, to a large extent formed the ethos, the guiding principle of whatever endeavour I choose to undertake. I therefore make it a matter of principle to always choose work that I'm passionate about and find inspiring, work that I can without a doubt give my 100 percent.

Working at at Code for Africa, the continent's largest network of open data and civic technology labs, funded by Google News Labs, World Bank Group and The Bill & Melinda Gates Foundation. I get to spend my days working with a multicultural team of software engineers, data analysts and data journalists spread across the continent, who are helping change the power dynamics that govern our societies by providing digital tools that allow fellow citizens to engage with those in positions of power.In my role, I get to creatively use design to develop solutions and craft digital experiences that leverage data to inform, inspire and hopefully shift perspectives and discourse on Africa.

In my free time Volunteering at Lakehub allows me to keep my finger on the pulse of Kisumu's emerging Tech scene while at the same time mentoring the next generation of Geeks, Tech enthusiasts and everyone in between. To quote Winston Churchill "You make a living by what you get, but you make a life by what you give"

Work & Education

July 2008 - June 2012

University of Nairobi

Bachelor of Commerce (2nd Class Upper Hons)

My Undergraduate studies is where I got my first proper introduction into the world of computing. My specialisation at the time quite new, merged the best of both worlds with a solid foundation in the business and management side of organization elegantly merged with the very rapidly changing world of technology and the risks and opportunities inherent

September 2018 - Present

Code For Africa

Data Vis Designer

Roles: Design data visualizations, info-graphics, maps and other visual devices for CfA’s storytelling and communication. Work with journalists and developers from across the continent to design and build engaging static and interactive visualizations and tools. Dive into the UI/UX of CfA’s interactive websites and tools to bring them in line with User needs as well as emerging trends. Harmonize the look and feel of CfA’s products, content and brands. Develop and maintain the team’s design guidelines. Occasionally take part in training and community events as a trainer and help review course ware created to teach data journalism

July 2019 - June 2019

CAFOD

Design Consultant

The Catholic Agency For Overseas Development (CAFOD) published a series of reports with the aim of bringing voices and examples from the ground into discussions about how to implement Agenda 2030 and the Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs) in a transformational way. The five reports are meant to help civil society organisations in their engagement with policy-makers at national and global level. My role was to layout and format the information contained in the reports in a logical yet visually engaging way such that the different audiences for which the reports were intended, could easily navigate and consume the information therein on print as well as digital media.

June 2019- June 2019

Data 4 Change

Interface Designer

My role in the Defy Hate Now project (a South Sudan based civic tech start-up combating the spread and impact of Fake news and other forms of Misinformation) was to develop the visual identity of SIFTA (Space for Ideas, Freedom, Transformation and Action) which was a multi-pronged initiative that developed interventions in Education, Policy, Technology and Strategic Partnerships with the objective of mitigating the impact of Fake News and Misinformation in fuelling conflict in South Sudan. Apart from the initiative's branding I also developed the User Interface for the technology intervention which was a platform that leveraged data from social media as well as other sources to showcase the reach, engagement and factfulness of content shared on the various media platforms in South Sudan

March 2020 - July 2020

Transnational Migration - Data Visualisation

Data Vis Designer

My Role in this project was to develop a data visualisation prototype based of the migration data. The goal of the project was to humanise the data traditionally visualised using networks in a narrative format that delves deeper into the history, motivations and connections that may be overlooked by the traditional foramt.

March 2020 - July 2020

Materenal Figures

UI Designer

Maternal Figures is a Project funded by the Columbia University with the objective of analysing the state of Maternal Healthcare in Nigeria. In this Project my role was to design the User interface for the data platform, which was essentially a website that collated data on the various aspects of the Maternal Healthcare infrastructure in Nigeria and from the data presented insights which would in turn be used by Researchers Journalists and policymakers to drive better informed decision making
